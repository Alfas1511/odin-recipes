Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich sauces, cheeses, and various fillings, then baking it to perfection. Traditionally, it features layers of béchamel or ricotta cheese, a hearty meat sauce made with ground beef or sausage, tomato sauce, and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan. The combination of flavors and textures—soft pasta, creamy cheese, and savory sauce—makes lasagna a comforting and satisfying meal that’s perfect for family gatherings or special occasions.

While the traditional version is beloved worldwide, lasagna has many delicious variations. Vegetarian versions often substitute meat with roasted vegetables, spinach, or lentils, while some recipes use different cheeses or even seafood. Whether served in a cozy home kitchen or an elegant restaurant, lasagna remains a symbol of warmth, togetherness, and the comforting essence of Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles (pasta sheets) – either fresh or dry
  • Ground beef or sausage – for the meat sauce (optional for vegetarian versions)
  • Olive oil – for sautéing
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste – to thicken the sauce
  • Salt and black pepper – to taste
  • Italian herbs (basil, oregano, thyme) – for flavor
  • Sugar (optional) – to balance the acidity of tomatoes

Steps for preparation

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce

    1. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onions and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
    3. Add ground beef or sausage, cooking until browned. Drain excess fat.
    4. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, salt, pepper, and Italian herbs (basil, oregano, thyme).
    5. Simmer for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and flavors blend.

  2. Prepare the Cheese Mixture

    1. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, 1 beaten egg, half of the shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, and chopped parsley.
    2. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Set aside.

  3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles

    1. Boil lasagna sheets in salted water according to package directions until al dente.
    2. Drain and lay them flat on a clean towel to prevent sticking.

  4. Assemble the Lasagna

    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Grease a baking dish with butter or olive oil.
    3. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
    4. Add a layer of noodles, then spread ricotta mixture, followed by meat sauce, and mozzarella cheese.
    5. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a generous layer of sauce and cheese on top.

  5. Bake

    1. Cover with foil (lightly sprayed with cooking oil to prevent sticking).
    2. Bake for 25–30 minutes.
    3. Remove foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes, until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

  6. Serve

    1. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting—this helps it set.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley, and serve warm.
